Using origami to teach young children science

  • Top Article
1 of 1
Write now Article Tools

by James Johnson

Children love to make things and they love to explore.  If you can combine these two factors, you can teach young children almost anything .  Science is, to a large extent, learning how things work and often how they can fit together.  Origami involves using you hands and paper to create three dimensional images.  Origami can be used to get young children started on an interest in science.

Start with origami like you should start with science, keep it simple.  Make a box or even a bird or paper airplane.  Then get the child to do the same thing.  The child will have to try several times to get it correct, but that is okay, you can explain that scientist rarely get anything right the first time!  You then start to move to more complex things.

What is important is that they learn that good science, like good origami, is paying attention to details.  One wrong fold or mistake can throw the whole design or experiment off and things don't work like they should.  This can be especially important in Chemistry and Physics.  Add a wrong chemical and either nothing happens, or something explodes (or becomes poisonous).  Build an airplane wrong and it crashes due to the physics involved!  With the paper, they will learn that some things will work better than others, true of science as well as origami!

Origami can also be used to teach biology.  Get the children to think about their favorite animal and then try to research everything they know about it and write it on paper.  This will teach them about the animals, how they live and what they do, but now have them try to fold it from the paper!  It was a lot better than just writing a report, then tossing the paper and you can tell the children that they are learning what all goes into an animal!

There are also origami that can be very specific, such as an origami molecule kit to teach the elements.  Insect origami will teach wonders about the difference in those species, and the children will learn without realizing what they are doing.  The same for sea creatures, birds and even dinosaurs.  Sound to complex for young children?  They can start doing it at 3, and will learn much quicker , and much more, than anyone expects!

Origami to teach science, it works!
